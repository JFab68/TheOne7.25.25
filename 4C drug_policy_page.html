Public Health Crisis

Beyond prison walls, our communities confront an escalating public health crisis. Opioid-related deaths have surged by an alarming 80% since 2017, devastating families and communities across Arizona. The continued criminalization of substance use disorders‚Äîtreating a health condition as a criminal matter‚Äîhas led to increased stigma, limited healthcare access, and cycles of incarceration rather than recovery.

These intersecting crises disproportionately impact vulnerable populations, including those with limited access to healthcare, legal resources, and support services. Without appropriate intervention and policy reform, these challenges create lasting harm that extends throughout communities and across generations.

Evidence-Based Solutions

Taking a public health approach to substance use, we work to expand access to naloxone, harm reduction supplies, and treatment resources that save lives. We advocate for policies that treat addiction as a health issue rather than a crime, reducing stigma and increasing opportunities for recovery.
